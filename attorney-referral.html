A Direct Referral Path for Family Law Attorneys

Neutral, real-estate-only guidance for your clients navigating property decisions during divorce or separation.

Why Attorneys Use This Referral Path

Time Efficiency

A single form connects your client directly. No follow-up calls or coordination required from your office.

Role Clarity

Real estate guidance stays separate from legal counsel. Clear boundaries are maintained throughout.

Neutral Positioning

Your client receives objective market information without advocacy or pressure to list.

What Your Client Receives

Real-estate-only services with no legal advice and no obligation to list.

  • Market analysis and property valuation context
  • Equity position and timing considerations
  • Documentation formatted for legal proceedings
  • Listing strategy consultation if appropriate
  • Coordination with counsel as needed

When Attorneys Typically Use This

Client needs current market value for negotiations or settlement
Court-ordered sale requires neutral listing agent
One party is uncooperative or communication is limited
Client is evaluating whether to retain or sell the property
Timeline coordination with court dates is required
High-conflict situation requires professional protocols

How the Referral Process Works

1
You Submit

Complete the referral form with your client's contact information and case context.

2
I Reach Out

Your client is contacted within 24 hours to schedule an initial conversation.

3
Client Decides

Your client determines next steps based on their needs. No obligation to proceed.

4
You Stay Informed

With client consent, updates and documentation can be shared with your office.

What This Is Not

Not legal advice or legal representation
Not a sales funnel or marketing lead capture
Not pressure to list or sell the property
Not an exclusive or ongoing referral arrangement

This is a professional handoff tool. Your client receives real estate guidance appropriate to their situation, with clear boundaries maintained throughout.

What I Provide

Documentation, market analysis, and professional coordination for family law matters involving real property.

Comparative Market Analysis

Data-supported property valuations for use in negotiations, mediations, and court proceedings.

Timeline Coordination

Alignment of listing, showing, and closing schedules with court dates and legal deadlines.

Neutral Representation

Professional listing services that maintain neutrality when both parties require cooperation.

Documentation Standards

Clear records of market conditions, pricing rationale, and transaction milestones for legal review.

How I Work With Legal Professionals

  • Direct communication with counsel as needed
  • Documentation formatted for court submission
  • Coordination with mediators and appointed professionals
  • Confidential handling of sensitive transactions
